About me
I am a French student at the École Nationale Supérieure de Mécanique et d'Aérotechnique (ISAE-ENSMA), which is what we call in France a 'Grande École'. I will be graduating in 2025 with a 'Diplôme d'Ingénieur', equivalent to an MSc in aerospace and aeronautics with a specialisation in computer science, and also an MSc in computer science for avionics.
The curriculum centers on computer science and encompasses various other disciplines, such as fluid and structural mechanics, aerodynamics, energetics, thermodynamics, propulsion, and materials.
To gain admission to this school, I completed a two-year program in a "Classe Préparatoire aux Grandes Écoles" (CPGE).
In brief, the CPGE consists of two intense years preparing students for the highly competitive entrance exams of the prestigious 'Grandes Écoles'. The curriculum covers a broad range of subjects, from pure mathematics to philosophy, and provides in-depth study.
With a long-standing passion for computer science, electronics, and the sciences, I have invested significant time in self-study, programming, and building projects, which has enabled me to explore various subjects. As a result, I have developed strong skills in creativity, autonomy, and curiosity, while expanding my scientific knowledge.

Distributed Computing

Basic pure C library for distributed computing with dedicated os.
Distributed computing refers to a network of computers working together to solve a problem or complete a task, instead of a single computer handling all the processing. The workload is divided among multiple machines, allowing for faster and more efficient processing.
The purpose of this project was more to learn about network optimizations than to code something revolutionary.

Neuroevolution of augmenting topologies (NEAT)

C++ library for evolving neural networks with NEAT. The purpose of this project was both a first step to make my own evolving neural network algorithm (e.g. PNEATM) and a way to learn more about C++ optimizations.
NEAT is a machine learning technique used to evolve artificial neural networks. This algorithm is often used for complex tasks such as game playing and robotics, where traditional hand-designed neural networks may not be effective.
